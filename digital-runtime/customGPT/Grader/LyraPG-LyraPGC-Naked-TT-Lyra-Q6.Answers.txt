When the Method Becomes Contamination: AI-Use Stigma and Evidence Displacement in Human–AI Workflows

Core claim:
Once people learn that AI was involved, they often stop evaluating the object and start evaluating the method. The AI label can contaminate the reception of the evidence, complaint, research note, testimony, transcript, or creative work.

This is adjacent to transcript integrity, but different.

This new paper asks:

Does the fact of AI involvement cause observers to misread, downgrade, pathologize, or procedurally dismiss the object?

Object custody is not only about what models do to objects. It is also about what institutions do to AI-mediated objects after they see the AI wrapper.

complaint → “AI-generated rant”
evidence summary → “not authentic”
mental-health transcript → “AI psychosis artifact”
accessibility aid → “cheating”
research method → “contamination”
legal draft → “not really claimant-authored”

A. human-written
B. AI-assisted
C. AI-generated from transcript
